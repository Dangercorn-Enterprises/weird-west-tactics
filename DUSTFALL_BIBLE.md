# DUSTFALL: THE ASHEN FRONTIER
## Game Design Bible v2.0
*For developers, artists, and AI asset generators*
*American Gods meets Final Fantasy Tactics in the sun-scorched frontier*

---

## ELEVATOR PITCH

The gods followed their people to America. Now they're at war, and the frontier is the battlefield. Dustfall is a turn-based tactical RPG with isometric pixel art, a divine faction system, and combat inspired by FFT — set in a steampunk western world being torn apart by warring deities.

---

## 1. WORLD & SETTING

### The Premise
The year is 1889. As waves of immigrants poured into America — Europeans, Africans, Asians, Latin Americans — their gods came with them. The Old World deities crossed the ocean in the hearts of their worshippers, drawn to the raw potential of the New World.

But something about the American frontier — its vastness, its violence, its desperate belief — gave these gods physical form. They didn't just influence. They **manifested**.

Now the frontier is a divine warzone. Gods compete for territory and worshippers. Their clashing influence warps the land, mutates creatures, and grants terrible powers to those who pledge allegiance. The fallout of divine conflict rains down as **Ashfall** — a luminous, screaming mineral that burns hotter than coal and powers impossible machines.

The native spirits of this land — Coyote, Raven, Thunderbird — were here first. They've been fighting to keep the Old World gods in check. They're losing.

You lead a crew of frontier survivors caught between warring gods. Every faction wants your loyalty. Every deity offers power at a price. The Ashlands don't care what you believe — only whether you're fast enough to survive.

### The Ashfall
When gods clash, the fallout is physical. **Ashfall** is the crystallized residue of divine conflict — a glowing blue-green mineral that:
- Burns hotter than coal and **screams** when ignited
- Powers impossible machinery (the steampunk tech layer)
- Corrupts living things with prolonged exposure
- Amplifies divine powers near deposits
- Is the most valuable and most dangerous substance on Earth

Think of it as nuclear fallout, but spiritual. The more the gods fight, the more Ashfall contaminates the land.

### Geography
- **The Flats** — Endless alkaline desert. Cracked earth, dust devils, bleached bones. Settlements cling to oases and rail lines. Contested ground — no single god dominates.
- **The Forgeworks** — Industrial canyon city built into mesa walls. Vulcan's stronghold. Factories belch steam. Clockwork automatons patrol. Ghost rock (Ashfall) refineries light up the night in blue-green fire.
- **The Boneyard** — Ancient burial ground where Baron Samedi's influence seeps up from below. The dead don't stay down. Tombstones lean at angles in shifting sand. A permanent twilight hangs over this region.
- **Thunderheart** — High desert plateau where lightning strikes constantly. Perun's domain. The marshals and lawkeepers train here. The air smells like ozone and justice.
- **The Trickster's Maze** — A region of impossible geography. Canyons that loop back on themselves. Mirages that are real. Coyote's playground. Outlaws and hexslingers thrive here.
- **Rustwater** — A frontier boomtown built around the last clean aquifer. Neutral ground by necessity — too many factions need the water. Saloons, machine shops, a marshal's office, and shrines to three different gods crammed into two blocks. The player's home base.
- **The Scorch** — A stretch of desert turned to glass by a divine battle decades ago. Twisted metal wreckage half-buried in sand. Ashfall deposits glow beneath the surface. The Sleeping One stirs here.

### The Pantheon

The gods of Dustfall are real, present, and at war. Each deity has a **domain**, a **faction** of mortal followers, and a **corruption** — the dark side of their influence.

| Deity | Origin | Domain | Faction | Corruption |
|-------|--------|--------|---------|------------|
| **Vulcan** | Roman | Forge, fire, industry | Forgeworks Syndicate | Dehumanization — people become parts |
| **Perun** | Slavic | Thunder, war, justice | The Regulators | Fanaticism — law without mercy |
| **Baron Samedi** | Haitian Vodou | Death, crossroads, resurrection | The Harrowed | Undeath — the living become fuel |
| **Coyote** | Native American | Trickery, change, the wild | The Dust Walkers | Chaos — reality breaks down |
| **Anansi** | West African | Stories, cunning, knowledge | Los Tejedores (The Weavers) | Manipulation — truth ceases to exist |
| **The Sleeping One** | Unknown/Primordial | ???, endings, the deep earth | The Hollow Court | Annihilation — everything returns to dust |

#### Vulcan — The Forgefather
The Roman god of fire and metalwork found paradise in America's industrial revolution. His influence powers the steampunk layer of the world — Ashfall refineries, clockwork automatons, steam-powered rail guns. His followers in the **Forgeworks Syndicate** are industrialists, engineers, and company men strip-mining the frontier. Vulcan doesn't hate humanity — he just sees everything as raw material.

#### Perun — The Thunderer
The Slavic god of thunder and righteous war. He crossed the ocean with Eastern European immigrants and found a land desperately in need of order. His followers are the **Regulators** — marshals, preachers, and honest folk who believe in law at any cost. Perun's blessing makes you strong, fast, and certain. The problem is certainty — his most devoted become zealots who see nuance as weakness.

#### Baron Samedi — The Gatekeeper
The Haitian Vodou loa of death arrived with enslaved Africans and their descendants. In the Ashlands, where death is common and Ashfall corrupts the boundary between life and death, the Baron's power runs deep. His **Harrowed** are the returned dead — some who fight the darkness inside, most who surrendered to it. The Baron isn't evil. He just doesn't see much difference between alive and dead.

#### Coyote — The Trickster
The oldest god in the Ashlands. Coyote was here before any immigrant deity, and he's not happy about the company. Shapeshifter, liar, teacher, fool — Coyote's **Dust Walkers** are indigenous spiritual warriors, outlaws, and anyone who values freedom over order. Coyote's influence makes the land strange — canyons that loop, mirages that bite, ghost towns that weren't there yesterday. His corruption is pure chaos.

#### Anansi — The Weaver
The West African spider god of stories and cunning. Anansi's power is information — secrets, leverage, the story you tell yourself about who you are. His faction, **Los Tejedores** ("The Weavers"), are spies, hexslingers, gamblers, and anyone who fights with their mind. Anansi doesn't care who wins. He cares who tells the best story.

#### The Sleeping One — The Final Threat
Something was in this land before any god, any spirit, any living thing. It sleeps beneath the Scorch, and the Ashfall is its dreaming. As the gods war above, their violence feeds it below. If it wakes, none of them survive. This is the campaign's ultimate threat — every faction has a reason to stop it, but none can agree on how.

### Tone & Atmosphere
- Oppressive heat. Everything is sun-bleached and sand-worn.
- Ashfall machinery hisses, clicks, and occasionally screams.
- Gods walk among mortals — sometimes disguised, sometimes blazing with power.
- Violence is consequential. People die ugly and stay dead (mostly).
- Dark humor lives alongside genuine dread.
- Divine influence is visible — Vulcan's territory has brass-and-steam architecture, Baron Samedi's has permanent twilight, Coyote's shifts and lies.
- Think: American Gods meets The Good, The Bad and The Ugly meets FF6's War of the Magi

## 2. ART DIRECTION

### Style Reference: SNES-Era 16-Bit
**Primary references:**
- **Final Fantasy VI** — Character sprites, world map tiles, battle UI. The War of the Magi parallels our gods-at-war premise.
- **Final Fantasy Tactics** — Isometric grid, height variation, camera feel
- **Chrono Trigger** — Environmental storytelling through sprite detail
- **Ogre Battle / Tactics Ogre** — Dark tone tactical sprite work

### Resolution & Scale
- **Tile size:** 64x32 pixels (isometric diamond)
- **Character sprites:** 32x48 pixels (standing), with 4 directional facings (N/S/E/W)
- **Animation frames:** 3-4 per action (idle, walk, attack, hit, death)
- **Palette:** Limited to ~32 colors per tileset/character for authentic 16-bit feel
- **Canvas render size:** 960x640 native, scaled to display

### Sprite Design Guidelines (for AI art generators)

#### Characters — General Rules
- 16-bit SNES pixel art style, NOT modern HD pixel art
- Characters should be ~3 heads tall (chibi proportions like FF6 field sprites)
- 4 directional sprites: front, back, left, right
- Visible silhouette must be distinct at 32x48 pixels — exaggerate signature elements
- Clothing is sun-worn, dusty, patched. Nothing is clean or new.
- Ashfall tech has visible tubing, brass fittings, and a faint blue-green glow
- Deity-aligned characters have subtle visual tells (Vulcan = brass accents, Perun = storm blue, etc.)

#### Environment Tiles — General Rules
- Isometric diamond projection (64x32 base tile)
- Height tiles stack vertically (+16 pixels per height level)
- Sand should have subtle dithering pattern (2-3 shades)
- Rock formations use hard pixel edges, not smooth gradients
- Ashfall veins pulse with 2-frame animation (dim/bright)
- Buildings reflect local deity influence (Vulcan towns = brass/steam, Perun towns = stone/iron)
- Cacti, dead trees, bleached skulls, and rusted machinery as scatter props

### Specific Asset Prompts (for Grok/Gemini)

**Character sprites — prompt template:**
> "16-bit SNES-style pixel art character sprite, 32x48 pixels, [ARCHETYPE DESCRIPTION], isometric RPG style like Final Fantasy VI, 4-directional facing, limited color palette, desert steampunk western setting with divine/mythological elements, transparent background"

**Tileset — prompt template:**
> "16-bit isometric tileset, 64x32 pixel diamond tiles, [TERRAIN TYPE], SNES RPG style like Final Fantasy Tactics, desert western setting with [DEITY] divine influence, steampunk elements, limited color palette, pixel art"

**UI elements — prompt template:**
> "16-bit pixel art RPG UI panel, [ELEMENT DESCRIPTION], parchment and brass aesthetic, western steampunk style with divine iconography, SNES era, transparent background"

## 3. COMBAT SYSTEM (FFT-Inspired)

### Grid
- **Isometric diamond grid**, 12x12 to 16x16 tiles (varies per mission)
- **Height system:** 0-4 levels per tile. Higher ground = accuracy bonus, harder to reach
- **Terrain types:**
  - Sand (open) — No modifier
  - Rocky ground — No modifier, can't dig
  - Full cover (crates, walls, boulders) — +40% defense
  - Half cover (cacti, barrels, fences) — +20% defense
  - Ashfall deposit — Glows, can explode if hit (AoE damage). Amplifies divine abilities in radius.
  - Water/quicksand — Costs 2 AP to cross
  - High ground — +10% accuracy per height level above target
  - Consecrated ground (shrine tile) — Deity-specific buff to aligned units
  - Corrupted ground (Sleeping One) — Damages all units each turn

### Turn System: Charge Time (CT) — Inspired by FFT
- Every unit has a **Speed** stat
- Each tick, every unit gains CT equal to their Speed
- When CT reaches **100**, that unit acts
- After acting, CT resets to **0** (or partial reset for Wait actions)
- This means fast units act more often — not just "my turn, your turn"
- **Turn order bar** displayed at top of screen showing upcoming 8-unit forecast

### Action Points (AP)
- Each unit gets AP based on archetype + Quickness stat
- **Base:** 3 AP + floor(Quickness / 4)
- Movement, attacks, and abilities all cost AP
- Unused AP does NOT carry over

### Actions
| Action | AP Cost | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Move (per tile) | 1 | +1 for climbing height, +1 for difficult terrain |
| Basic Attack | 2 | Weapon-dependent range and accuracy |
| Quick Attack | 1 | -15% accuracy, available for some weapons |
| Ability | 2-3 | Archetype-specific special moves |
| Divine Ability | 3 | Patron deity power. Stronger near shrines. |
| Hunker Down | 2 | +20% defense until next turn |
| Overwatch | 2 | Free attack when enemy enters weapon range |
| Use Item | 1 | Consumables (bandages, Ashfall charges, dynamite) |
| Reload | 1 | Some weapons require reloading after X shots |
| Wait | 0 | End turn, gain +10 CT bonus next cycle |

### Damage Formula
```
raw_damage = random(weapon.min, weapon.max) + floor(Strength / 3)
accuracy = weapon.accuracy + (Deftness - 5) x 3 - (target.Nimbleness - 5) x 2
accuracy += height_advantage x 10
accuracy -= cover_bonus
accuracy -= distance_penalty (beyond effective range)
accuracy = clamp(5%, 95%)

if hit:
  damage = raw_damage - floor(target.Vigor / 4)
  if target.hunkered: damage -= 3
  if divine_advantage: damage x 1.25 (patron deity strong vs target's deity)
  damage = max(1, damage)

if critical (roll > 90% of accuracy threshold):
  damage x 1.5, ignore cover
```

### Death & Injury
- At 0 HP, unit is **Downed** (3-turn bleed-out timer, like FFT)
- Allies can **Revive** with items or abilities before timer expires
- If timer expires: **Dead**. Permanent in campaign mode.
- Baron Samedi-aligned enemies can raise the fallen as Harrowed...
- Divine patronage can offer one "miracle save" per campaign — but the deity remembers the debt

## 4. CHARACTER SYSTEM

### Divine Patronage
Every recruitable character can align with a deity. Patronage provides:
- **1 Divine Ability** unique to that god (costs 3 AP, powerful)
- **Passive bonus** in that god's territory
- **Faction reputation** bonuses with aligned NPCs
- **Corruption risk** — overuse of divine power inches toward the god's dark side

Patronage can be **changed** at shrines, but it costs reputation and there's a 3-mission cooldown with no divine abilities while the old god's influence fades and the new one takes hold.

**Divine Abilities by Patron:**
| Patron | Divine Ability | Effect |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Vulcan | **Forge Strike** | Superheated melee attack, ignores armor. 8-14 dmg. |
| Perun | **Thunderbolt** | Ranged lightning. 6-12 dmg, guaranteed hit, stun 1 turn. |
| Baron Samedi | **Death's Touch** | Drain: deal 5-8 dmg, heal self 100%. Can target downed units to raise as temporary ally. |
| Coyote | **Mirage** | Create illusory copy of self. Decoy draws attacks for 2 turns. |
| Anansi | **Weaver's Curse** | Target believes allies are enemies for 1 turn. Confused unit attacks nearest. |

### 6 Archetypes

#### Gunslinger
- **Fantasy:** The fastest draw in the Ashlands
- **Sprite cues:** Wide-brimmed hat, duster coat, twin revolvers on hips
- **Strengths:** High accuracy, multi-shot abilities, good AP economy
- **Abilities:**
  - *Fan the Hammer* (3 AP) — Fire 3 shots at -10% accuracy each
  - *Dead Eye* (2 AP) — Next shot is guaranteed crit if it hits
  - *Quick Draw* (passive) — +15 CT at start of combat

#### Hexslinger
- **Fantasy:** Gambler who channels divine power through cards and cunning
- **Sprite cues:** Playing cards tucked in hatband, one glowing hand, vest and shirtsleeves
- **Strengths:** Ranged magic damage, debuffs, soul drain sustain
- **Abilities:**
  - *Hex Bolt* (2 AP) — Ranged magic attack, ignores cover
  - *Soul Drain* (2 AP) — Damage an enemy, heal self for 50% of damage dealt
  - *Jinx* (1 AP) — Target gets -20% accuracy for 2 turns

#### Tinkerer
- **Fantasy:** Mad scientist blessed by Vulcan's forge-fire
- **Sprite cues:** Goggles on forehead, tool belt, mechanical arm or backpack device, brass and copper
- **Strengths:** Area control, turret deployment, explosive gadgets
- **Abilities:**
  - *Deploy Turret* (3 AP) — Place auto-firing turret (8 HP, 4 range, 2-5 dmg)
  - *Ashfall Grenade* (2 AP) — AoE damage in 2-tile radius, 4-8 dmg
  - *Overclock* (1 AP) — Ally gains +2 AP this turn

#### Preacher
- **Fantasy:** Fire-and-brimstone servant of Perun's divine justice
- **Sprite cues:** White collar, black coat, holy book in off-hand, shotgun
- **Strengths:** Healing, buffs, anti-undead damage bonus
- **Abilities:**
  - *Lay on Hands* (2 AP) — Heal ally 5-10 HP, range 2
  - *Smite* (2 AP) — +50% damage vs Harrowed/Undead
  - *Sanctuary* (3 AP) — All allies in 3-tile radius gain +20% defense for 2 turns

#### Law Dog
- **Fantasy:** The last honest marshal, channeling Perun's thunder
- **Sprite cues:** Star badge on chest, rifle slung on back, sturdy boots, storm-blue accents
- **Strengths:** Tanky, long range, leadership buffs
- **Abilities:**
  - *Hold the Line* (2 AP) — +30% defense for self, taunt nearby enemies
  - *Rallying Cry* (2 AP) — All allies gain +10% accuracy for 2 turns
  - *Called Shot* (3 AP) — Guaranteed hit, can target specific body part (leg = slow, arm = disarm)

#### Drifter
- **Fantasy:** Nobody knows where they came from. Coyote smiles when they walk by.
- **Sprite cues:** Poncho/serape, wide hat pulled low, bandolier, knife on belt
- **Strengths:** High mobility, stealth, versatile but not specialized
- **Abilities:**
  - *Dust Devil* (2 AP) — Move through enemies without triggering overwatch
  - *Ambush* (passive) — First attack from stealth deals +50% damage
  - *Scavenge* (1 AP) — Search adjacent tile for items/ammo

### Stats (9 Core)
1. **Deftness** — Ranged accuracy
2. **Nimbleness** — Dodge, evasion, speed
3. **Strength** — Melee damage, carry weight
4. **Quickness** — Speed/CT gain, AP pool
5. **Vigor** — HP pool, damage resistance
6. **Cognition** — Perception, ability accuracy
7. **Knowledge** — Crafting, gadget effectiveness
8. **Mien** — Leadership, intimidation, divine affinity
9. **Spirit** — Willpower, corruption resistance

### Progression (Campaign)
- **XP** earned per mission (kills, objectives, survival, deity-aligned choices)
- **Level up:** Choose +1 to any two stats, or +1 to one stat and learn a new ability
- **Divine progression:** Using patron abilities builds favor. High favor unlocks stronger divine abilities.
- **Gear:** Found as loot, purchased in towns, or crafted at Forges
- **Injuries:** Failed death saves can result in permanent stat penalties or lost limbs (replaceable with Ashfall prosthetics at any Forge)
- **Corruption:** Overusing divine power without rest pushes toward the patron's dark side. At high corruption, abilities become stronger but the character makes occasional AI-controlled actions reflecting the deity's will, not yours.

## 5. OVERWORLD MAP

### Design: FFT-Style Node Map with Divine Zones

The overworld is a **hand-drawn parchment map** viewed top-down, with:
- **Named locations** (towns, outposts, ruins, shrines, dungeons) as interactive nodes
- **Paths** connecting locations — some safe, some dangerous
- **Divine influence zones** — regions of the map tinted by the dominant deity's color. These shift as the story progresses.
- **Fog of war** — new locations revealed as you explore or hear rumors in saloons
- **Random encounters** — traveling dangerous paths may trigger optional combat encounters
- **Travel time** — moving between nodes costs in-game time. Some quests are time-limited.

### Map Flow
```
                    [The Forgeworks]
                    (Vulcan zone)
                        |
                   [Iron Pass]---[Copper Springs]
                        |              |
    [The Maze]---[Dusty Crossroads]---[Rustwater]---[Thunder Mesa]
    (Coyote)          |            (Neutral)        (Perun zone)
                  [The Scorch]
                  (Contested)
                      |
               [The Boneyard]---[Gallows Hill]
               (Samedi zone)        |
                              [The Deep Rift]
                              (Sleeping One)
```

### Path Types
| Path | Travel Time | Risk | Notes |
|------|-------------|------|-------|
| Maintained road | 1 day | Low | Towns, patrols |
| Desert trail | 2 days | Medium | Random encounters possible |
| Wilderness | 3 days | High | Encounters likely, harder enemies |
| Divine corridor | 1 day | Varies | Fast travel through a deity's domain — safe IF you're aligned, hostile if not |

### Mounts (from Stables)
| Mount | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| Frontier Horse | 200g | -1 day travel time |
| Desert Runner (camel) | 350g | -1 day, immune to sandstorm delays |
| Clockwork Steed | 600g | -2 days travel, never tires, requires Ashfall fuel |
| Coyote's Gift (spirit mount) | Free | Instant travel in Coyote's territory. Unpredictable elsewhere. |

## 6. TOWN & HUB MECHANICS

### Building Types

Every settlement has a subset of buildings based on its size and deity alignment. You interact with towns via a **menu-driven hub** (like FFT or Fire Emblem) — not a walkable town map. Clean, fast, focused.

---

#### The Saloon (Every Town)
The social hub. Three functions:
- **Rest** — Heal all units to full HP. Costs 1 day of travel time. Free.
- **Recruitment** — Hire mercenaries to fill your roster. Available mercs vary by town and faction. Higher-reputation towns offer better recruits.
- **Rumors** — Bartender shares intel. Unlocks side missions, reveals hidden map locations, warns about upcoming threats. Some rumors cost gold.

*Flavor:* Piano music, crowd noise, wanted posters on the wall. The bartender knows everything and charges accordingly.

---

Combined weapons, armor, and gear shop. One vendor, frontier-style.

**Weapons:**

| Shotgun | 3 | 6-12 | 65% | Devastating close, bad far |

| Steam Cannon | 6 | 7-12 | 55% | Heavy, Tinkerer only |

**Armor:**

---

#### The Forge (Uncommon — Vulcan-aligned towns, larger settlements)
Tinkerer's workshop and blacksmith combined. Material-based crafting.

**Services:**
- **Weapon Modification** — Add mods to weapons using materials
  - *Ashfall Chamber* (+2 max damage, weapon glows blue-green)
  - *Hair Trigger* (+5% accuracy, -1 min damage)
  - *Extended Barrel* (+1 range, -5% accuracy)
  - *Hollow Points* (+3 damage vs unarmored, -2 vs armored)
  - *Divine Engraving* (weapon deals patron deity's damage type)
- **Armor Enhancement** — Reinforce or add properties
  - *Ashfall Lining* (+1 defense, +corruption resist)
  - *Insulated Plating* (+fire/lightning resist)
  - *Servo Joints* (remove speed penalty from heavy armor)
- **Gadget Crafting** (Tinkerer-specific)
  - Turret upgrades (range, damage, HP)
  - Deployable cover (place half-cover on battlefield)
  - Ashfall scanner (reveal hidden enemies/items on map)
- **Prosthetics** — Replace lost limbs with Ashfall-powered mechanical ones. Different prosthetics give different stat bonuses.

**Materials** are found as loot, bought from outfitters, or scavenged by Drifters:
- Scrap Metal (common) — Basic mods
- Ashfall Crystal (uncommon) — Energy mods
- Divine Ember (rare) — Deity-specific enhancements
- Sleeping Shard (very rare) — Powerful but corrupting

---

#### The Shrine (Uncommon — each shrine is deity-specific)
Where mortals commune with their patron god. Each town's shrine belongs to a specific deity.

**Services:**
- **Pray** — Receive a temporary blessing for the next mission (+stat buff based on deity)
  - Vulcan: +2 Knowledge, +1 damage with tech weapons
  - Perun: +2 Vigor, +1 defense
  - Baron Samedi: +2 Spirit, lifesteal on first kill
  - Coyote: +2 Nimbleness, guaranteed dodge on first attack
  - Anansi: +2 Cognition, reveal all enemies at mission start
- **Change Patronage** — Switch a character's deity alignment. Costs 500g and 3 missions of no divine abilities during transition.
- **Increase Favor** — Donate gold or materials to increase standing with the deity. High favor unlocks stronger divine abilities and faction perks.
- **Purify** — Reduce corruption level. Costs gold and time.

*Flavor:* Each shrine looks different. Vulcan's is a forge-altar of hammered brass. Perun's is a lightning-struck oak tree. Baron Samedi's is a crossroads marked with skulls and rum bottles. Coyote's is a circle of stones that may or may not have been there yesterday.

---

#### The Stables (Uncommon — frontier towns)
Mounts for overworld travel. See Mount table in Section 5.

**Additional services:**
- **Horse Care** — Heal/restore injured mounts (yes, mounts can be injured in ambushes)
- **Mount Training** — Improve mount stats over time (multiple visits)
- **Trade** — Swap mounts, sell unwanted ones

---

#### Doc's Office (Rare — larger towns only)
Medical services beyond battlefield bandages.

**Services:**
- **Heal Injuries** — Permanent stat penalties from failed death saves can be reduced (not fully removed) for gold + time
- **Cure Corruption** — More effective than shrine purification, but expensive
- **Tonics** — Buy medical consumables at a discount
- **Autopsy** — Examine a fallen enemy type to learn weaknesses (+permanent accuracy bonus vs that enemy type)

---

#### Marshal's Office (Rare — Perun-aligned towns)
Law enforcement hub.

**Services:**
- **Bounty Board** — Side missions: hunt specific outlaws/creatures for gold + XP. Bounties have time limits.
- **Reputation** — View your standing with each faction. Some bounties improve one faction's rep while hurting another.
- **Prisoner Exchange** — Sometimes captured enemies can be turned in for rewards or information.
- **Posse** — For very difficult bounties, hire a temporary NPC ally from the marshal's roster.

---

### Town Mood & Faction Influence
Each town has a **mood** that reflects recent events:
- **Thriving** — Full stock, better recruits, cheaper prices
- **Stable** — Normal
- **Tense** — Limited stock, some services unavailable, random faction events
- **Under Siege** — Defense mission may trigger, services minimal

Player actions in the story affect town moods. Helping a town's faction improves their mood. Ignoring threats or siding with enemies worsens it.

## 8. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Act 1 — Rustwater (5 missions)
*Theme: Introduction. Learn the factions. Choose your first allegiance.*
1. **The Claim** — Tutorial. Defend your mining claim from coyotes and a dust devil.
2. **Unwelcome Visitors** — Outlaws raid Rustwater. Urban combat in the streets.
3. **The Old Mine** — Explore abandoned Ashfall mine. First undead encounter. Baron Samedi's influence seeps up.
4. **Company Men** — Forgeworks Syndicate arrives. Diplomacy or violence. First major faction choice.
5. **Night of the Dead** — Boneyard erupts. Defend Rustwater. Boss: **The Deacon**. First divine power awakening.

### Act 2 — The Frontier (5 missions)
*Theme: Exploration. The gods make their moves. Alliances tested.*
6. **The Crossing** — Travel through the Trickster's Maze. Sandstorm hazard tiles. Coyote toys with you.
7. **The Wreck** — Explore crashed Ashfall locomotive in the Scorch. Golem encounter. Signs of the Sleeping One.
8. **Thunderheart Summit** — Meet Perun's champions at Thunder Mesa. Prove yourself or be cast out. Choice mission.
9. **The Forgeworks** — Infiltrate Vulcan's factory-temple. Heavy automaton presence. Moral choice: destroy or seize.
10. **The Iron Foreman** — Boss fight against Vulcan's war machine in open desert.

### Act 3 — The Reckoning (4 missions)
*Theme: The Sleeping One stirs. Gods must unite or die. Your choices determine who stands with you.*
11. **Into the Rift** — Descend into the Scorch's fissure. The Sleeping One's corruption is everywhere.
12. **The Divine Concord** — Attempt to unite the gods. Which ones answer depends on your faction choices. Failed diplomacy = fighting a god's champion instead.
13. **The Hollow Man** — Boss fight. The Sleeping One's herald. The dead king of the Ashlands.
14. **Dustfall** — Final mission. Seal the rift before the Sleeping One wakes. Your deity patron grants ultimate power. Multiple endings based on: which gods aided you, your corruption level, who survived.

### Mission Types
- **Assault** — Kill all enemies
- **Defense** — Survive X turns, protect objective
- **Escort** — Guide NPC across map
- **Stealth** — Avoid detection, reach extraction
- **Boss** — Multi-phase boss encounter
- **Choice** — Branching mission where faction alignment determines enemies and allies

### Side Missions (from Saloon Rumors + Bounty Board)
- **Bounty Hunts** — Track and eliminate specific targets
- **Ashfall Runs** — Secure mining deposits before rival factions
- **Rescue** — Save captured NPCs (potential recruits)
- **Divine Trials** — Shrine-specific challenges that increase patron favor
- **The Weaver's Games** — Anansi's puzzle-missions with unusual win conditions

## 9. AUDIO DIRECTION

### Music
- **Overworld/menus:** Sparse acoustic guitar, desert wind ambiance, distant coyote howls
- **Town hubs:** Saloon piano, crowd murmur, horse hooves on dirt
- **Combat:** Ennio Morricone-style tension (twangy electric guitar, whistling, percussion)
- **Boss fights:** Industrial percussion + divine choir + Ashfall screaming SFX
- **Divine moments:** Each deity has a leitmotif — Vulcan (hammer percussion), Perun (rolling thunder), Samedi (jazz funeral), Coyote (coyote howl + laughter), Anansi (plucked strings like a web)
- **The Scorch:** Wind, creaking metal, distant thunder, silence. The Sleeping One's theme is just a heartbeat, deep and slow.

### Sound Effects
- Ashfall: high-pitched whine when activated, low hum when idle
- Gunshots: punchy, reverberant (wide open spaces)
- Divine abilities: each god has a distinct sound — thunder crack, forge ring, bone rattle, wind whisper, spider chittering
- Automaton movement: hiss of steam, click of gears, brass on stone
- Undead: dry rasping, bone cracking, shuffling sand

- **Deity system:** Global faction reputation tracker, corruption tracker per unit
